Overview

Zero Stealth is a funny stealth game with ragdoll physics we made during university. The objective was to complete a small game using Unity, which later would be evaluated by a jury composed by profesional game developers and journalists. I took different roles as we had less than 2 months, and we also had to pitch it and propose a fictional monetization plan for it.

Some of my responsabilities:

  • Designed the layout of the first level and most of the third one.
  • Designed the puzzle and interactions to solve it.
  • Design and coding the plattforms and laser corridors.
  • Code for the ragdoll.
  • Ducks behaviour and coding.
  • Model and animate the ducks!
  • 2D art for proposed transmedia collaborations on monetization.
  • LOTS MORE!

Game Synopsis

Agent Garcia embarks on the dangerous mission of discovering in which malevolent project the Zero Gravity study has submerged after his suspicious first success. Our protagonist must venture into the secret facilities known as ZG and discover the nature of this new (and Machiavellian) project.

Zero Stealth is a stealth, puzzle and humor game. In it, we take the role of Agent Garcia, using his "skills" in stealth, exploration and undercover operations. We can avoid the guards, run away from them when they discover us, and use the latest in DAC (Camouflage Absurd Devices).

Game Features

Avoid the guards

The security and employees of Zero Gravity won't hesitate to hit Agent Garcia with all their strength.

Some cammouflaging devices will work on some enemies but not on others: the cows may confuse you among other ducks, and the guards may be fooled by a Zero Gravity cap while the ZG employees will recognize each other.

Evade the traps

The smart criminal minds at Zero Gravity have filled their office with all sorts of dangers. Finding their workplace may prove difficult and probably will be filled with lava, lasers and security guards.
